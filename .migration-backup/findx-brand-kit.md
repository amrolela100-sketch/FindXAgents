# FindX Brand & Messaging Kit

## 1. Brand Positioning

### Positioning Statement

For Dutch SMBs and entrepreneurs who struggle with customer acquisition, **FindX** is the AI-powered prospecting agent that discovers your ideal customers and reaches out personally — so you close deals faster than with any manual method. Unlike generic CRM tools or expensive agencies, FindX combines deep Netherlands business-data intelligence with AI-personalized outreach that feels handcrafted for every prospect.

### Brand Name Validation

**FindX** works across both Dutch and English:
- **"Find"** — universal verb for discovery, immediately clear in both languages
- **"X"** — the unknown variable, the target, the next customer
- Phonetics: clean, one syllable per part (/fIndEks/ in Dutch, /faIndEks/ in English)
- Domain-friendly: findx.nl, findx.io, usefindx.com
- No negative connotations in Dutch or English

### Brand Voice

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Confident** | We know what works; no hedging | "FindX vindt je volgende klant" |
| **Direct** | Dutch-friendly bluntness; skip fluff | "Meer omzet, minder moeite" |

**Tone spectrum:** Lean slightly formal for initial outreach (respectful of Dutch business norms), relax to conversational once a relationship starts.

### Key Differentiators

1. **Netherlands-first data** — Native integration with KVK, Google Maps, and Dutch directories
2. **AI-personalized at scale** — Every email references specific, verified insights about the prospect's business
3. **Full pipeline, zero manual work** — From lead discovery to booked meeting, fully automated
4. **Actionable analysis** — We don't just find businesses; we identify exactly what they need and articulate it
5. **Dutch-market tuned** — Language, tone, timing, and regulations all calibrated for NL

## 3. Dutch Language Considerations

### Language Strategy

| Audience Segment | Primary Language | Rationale |
|-----------------|------------------|-----------|
| Local SMBs (eigenaar-directeur) | Dutch | Builds trust; most comfortable |
| Tech / SaaS companies | English | Industry norm in NL tech |
| International-oriented businesses | English | Matches their customer-facing language |
| Horeca / retail / bouw | Dutch | Sector norm; personal touch matters |

**Default: Dutch.** Switch to English only when the prospect's existing materials indicate international orientation.

### Dutch Business Communication Tone

1. **Use "je/jij" (informal "you")** — Modern Dutch business culture, especially in SME/tech, has moved away from "u." Using "je" signals approachability without disrespect. Exception: very traditional sectors (accountancy, legal) may warrant "u."

2. **Be direct** — Dutch culture values straightforwardness. Say what you mean, skip excessive pleasantries. A brief "hoe gaat het?" is fine; don't overdo small talk.

3. **Keep it short** — Dutch professionals are busy and appreciate brevity. Aim for 100-150 words per cold email.

4. **Show, don't tell** — Data and specifics over superlatives. Instead of "de beste service," say "73% meer aanvragen."

### Subject Line Strategies for NL Market

- **Personalization:** Include contact name or company name ({{contactName}}, {{companyName}})
- **Curiosity gap:** Reference a specific insight without revealing everything
- **Direct benefit:** "Meer klanten uit {{city}}"
- **Avoid:** ALL CAPS, exclamation marks, "GRATIS," emoji in subject lines (feels spammy in NL)
- **Optimal length:** 35-55 characters (mobile-friendly)
- **Timing:** Send Tue-Thu, 9:00-10:30 CET (peak email-checking times in NL)

### Regulatory Notes

- GDPR-compliant outreach: Always include unsubscribe option
- Dutch Telecom/Spamwet: Clear sender identification required
- KVK data is public but using it for direct marketing requires a legitimate interest basis
- Always offer opt-out in every email

## 5. Content Style Guide

### Terminology

| Use | Do not Use | Reason |
|-----|------------|--------|
| lead | prospect (in Dutch copy) | More common in NL sales context |
| klant | customer | Dutch-first |
| aanvraag | conversion | Clearer for SMB audience |
| online zichtbaarheid | web presence | Dutch-first, more natural |
| e-mail | mail / email | Formal consistency |
| analyse | review (noun) | Clearer in NL business context |
| AI | kunstmatige intelligentie | Abbreviation preferred in NL tech |
| gesprek | call | Dutch-first unless tech audience |
| afspraak | meeting | Dutch-first |
| pipeline | pijplijn | Use English term — universally adopted |

### Formatting Standards

1. **Headers:** Sentence case in Dutch (only first word + proper nouns capitalized)
2. **Numbers:** Use comma for decimals (3,5%) and period for thousands (1.000 leads)
3. **Currency:** Always EUR, never USD
4. **Dates:** DD Month YYYY (28 maart 2026), never 03/28/2026
5. **Time:** 24-hour format (14:30, not 2:30 PM)
6. **Lists:** Use bullet points for features, numbered for steps
7. **Bold:** Use for key terms, CTAs, and data points — not for emphasis
8. **Links:** Descriptive text, never "klik hier" alone

### Personalization Best Practices

1. **Always reference something specific** — Never send an email that could apply to any company. The {{specificInsight}} variable is mandatory.
2. **Local references work** — Mentioning the city, a local competitor, or regional market data increases response rates.
3. **Quantify when possible** — "30% meer aanvragen" beats "meer aanvragen."
4. **Mirror their language** — If a prospect's website uses English, switch to English outreach.
5. **Industry-specific language** — Use sector terminology naturally. For horeca: "bezetting"; for bouw: "offertes"; for retail: "voetverkeer."

### Email Formatting Rules

- Max 150 words for cold outreach
- Max 80 words for follow-ups
- Single CTA per email
- Plain text preferred over HTML for initial outreach (higher deliverability, feels personal)
- Signature: Name + role + FindX + one link

### Channel-Specific Guidelines

| Channel | Tone | Length | CTA Style |
|---------|------|--------|-----------|
| Cold email | Professional-direct | 100-150 words | Soft (meeting/analysis) |
| LinkedIn | Conversational | 300 chars (InMail) | Connection + value |
| Landing page | Confident-helpful | Concise sections | Strong (start trial) |
| Follow-up email | Brief-friendly | 50-80 words | Low friction |
| Blog / content | Educational | 800-1200 words | Inform, then convert |
